🇦🇺 Australia

Intakes, deadlines, visa requirements, and top universities for students applying to Australian institutions.

Intakes & Deadlines

Primary Intake

February 2026 Is Australia's Main Intake — Apply October–November 2025

The February intake is Australia's largest, offering the widest course selection, most scholarships, and best alignment with internship and hiring seasons. Applications typically close October–November 2025. Start preparing 12–15 months before your intended start date.

ANU

Australian National University International Applications Close December 15, 2025

ANU's international undergraduate applications for Semester 1, 2026 closed December 15, 2025. Overseas students must accept their offer by January 15, 2026. Semester 2 applications are open until May 15, 2026.

July Intake

July 2026 Intake Now Open — Apply December 2025 to April 2026

Australia's secondary intake offers flexibility for students who missed February deadlines. Strong participation from University of Melbourne, University of Sydney, UNSW, and Monash. Mid-year scholarships and smaller cohorts are advantages of the July intake.

Record Enrolment

201,490 International Students Arrived in Australia in February 2025 — Up 15%

Australia's international student intake hit a record high in February 2025, exceeding even pre-pandemic 2019 levels by 10%. Competition for spots at top institutions like University of Melbourne, University of Sydney, UNSW, and Monash is intensifying significantly.

Visa

Student Visa (Subclass 500) — Apply at Least 3 Months Before Course Start

For February 2026 intake, visa applications must be submitted by November 2025. Processing times have increased — apply immediately after receiving your offer letter. You'll need proof of enrollment, health insurance (OSHC), and financial capacity.

🇳🇿 New Zealand

Key dates, top universities, and visa guidance for students applying to New Zealand institutions.

Intakes & Deadlines

Primary Intake

February Is NZ's Primary Intake — Apply August–November 2025

New Zealand's February intake (Semester 1) offers the widest range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Applications typically open August–September and close November–December. This intake also has the most scholarships and the largest international student community.

2025–2026

Read more

July Intake

July 2026 Intake — Apply February to May 2026

The July intake is NZ's secondary option, popular among students who need extra preparation time or missed February deadlines. Most major universities including University of Auckland, Victoria University of Wellington, and Otago participate.

2025–2026

Read more

University of Auckland

Auckland Undergraduate Semester 2 International Deadline — July 4, 2025

The University of Auckland's undergraduate deadline for international students for Semester 2, 2025 was July 4. For 2026 Semester 1, apply by November 2025. Auckland is NZ's highest-ranked university, consistently in the global top 100.

Visas, Work & Scholarships

Post-Study Work

NZ Post-Study Work Visa Allows Up to 3 Years After Graduation

International graduates from recognized NZ institutions can apply for a post-study work visa of up to 3 years depending on qualification level. Students can also work up to 20 hours per week during semester and full-time during scheduled breaks.

Scholarships

NZ Excellence Awards Deadline — October 2025 for February 2026 Intake

The New Zealand Excellence Awards (NZEA) and university-specific scholarships worth up to NZ$15,000 are available. Scholarship deadlines often precede admission deadlines by 6–12 months. Victoria University Wellington has strong international scholarship offerings.

Visa Tightening

NZ Tightens Student Visa Standards — SOPs Now Under Greater Scrutiny

Immigration New Zealand has raised its standards for student visa applications. Statements of Purpose must clearly demonstrate genuine study intent. Students are advised to have documents professionally reviewed and to apply well in advance of course start dates.
