Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer in-person tutoring in Toronto and the GTA?
Yes. Neil offers in-person sessions in Toronto and the Greater Toronto Area, including North York, Markham, Thornhill, and Richmond Hill. In-person availability depends on scheduling — contact Neil to discuss what works for your location and timeline.
Can Canadian students take the SAT and ACT for US university admissions?
Absolutely — and many do. Canadian students applying to US universities are treated the same as American applicants for test score purposes. The SAT and ACT are offered at test centres across the GTA, including in Toronto, Markham, and surrounding areas. Neil advises GTA students on the full US admissions process, not just test prep.
Is the SAT or ACT better for GTA students?
There's no universal answer — it depends on the student's strengths. The Ontario curriculum aligns somewhat more naturally with ACT Math (which goes up to trigonometry, similar to the Ontario Grade 12 Advanced Functions course). However, many GTA students perform better on the SAT due to its less time-pressured format. Neil can advise after a short diagnostic session.
Do Canadian universities require the SAT or ACT?
Generally, no. Most Canadian universities — UofT, UBC, McGill, Queen's, Western — admit students based on Ontario high school grades, not SAT/ACT scores. However, some competitive programs and scholarship applications may consider them. The SAT/ACT is primarily valuable for students targeting US universities.
What is the typical timeline for SAT or ACT prep?
Most students benefit from 8–12 weeks of preparation. For students in Grade 10 or 11 targeting US universities, starting early gives the most flexibility — including the option to retake. Neil recommends beginning prep at least 3 months before your target test date.
Do you also help with university admissions counselling?
Yes. Beyond test prep, Neil offers full university admissions counselling for both undergraduate and MBA programs — school selection, essay strategy, application review, and interview preparation. His students have been admitted to Harvard, Stanford, Yale, UPenn, UChicago, Northwestern, University of Toronto, UBC, McGill, Oxford, and many other institutions.
