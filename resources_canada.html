Latest Canadian Admissions News

Essential deadlines and developments for students applying to Canadian universities in 2025–2026.

Key Deadlines

OUAC Ontario

Ontario Universities Application Deadline — January 15, 2026

Ontario secondary school students must submit OUAC applications by January 15, 2026 for Fall 2026 admission. Admission decisions are released in rounds starting January through late May. Some programs have earlier deadlines — check each university directly.

University of Toronto

U of T Early Consideration: Apply by November 7, Docs by December 2

Students who applied by November 7, 2025 and submitted supporting documents by December 2 were eligible for U of T's first admission round. Regular decisions continue through late May 2026. U of T offers admission across multiple rounds.

2025–2026 Cycle

Read more

Carleton University

Carleton International Deadline: January 15, 2026 for Fall Admission

Carleton University's OUAC deadline for Ontario high school students is January 15, 2026. International students should apply earlier — the January 2026 term deadline for internationals was October 15, 2025. Fall 2026 applications are now open.

Top Universities

UBC Vancouver

UBC Applications Open — Strong Growth in International Applicants

UBC Vancouver remains Canada's second-ranked university globally. For Fall 2026, early application is recommended. UBC uses a holistic review considering grades, activities, and personal profile essays. Many competitive programs have higher grade cutoffs than listed minimums.

McGill University

McGill Remains Among Canada's Most Competitive — Apply Early

McGill's competitive programs in Medicine, Engineering, Law, and Business regularly see cutoff grades above 90%. The university attracts a large international applicant pool. Application opens in fall; deadlines vary by program from November through January.

uOttawa

University of Ottawa 2026 Applications Opened September 18, 2025

Applications for programs beginning in 2026 opened September 18, 2025. International students should note Engineering and Sciences do not offer Winter term admissions. Apply well before the OUAC deadline for the best chance at competitive programs.

International Students & Visas

Visa Policy

Canada Tightening Student Visa Rules — Higher Rejection Rates in 2024–25

International students are facing increased scrutiny in Canadian study permit applications. Tighter policies introduced in 2024–25 have led to higher rejection rates. Students must demonstrate strong ties to home country, sufficient funds, and genuine study intent in their SOPs.

Study Permit

Apply for Study Permit at Least 3–4 Months Before Start Date

Canadian study permit processing times have increased significantly. International students should apply for their permit immediately after receiving an offer letter. Fall 2026 students should aim to apply by April–May 2026 at the latest.

Intakes

Canada's Three Intake Seasons: Fall, Winter & Summer

Fall (September) is the primary and most competitive intake, offering the widest program selection and largest scholarship pools. Winter (January) is a strong secondary option especially for graduate programs. Summer (May) is limited to specific programs.

Graduate & MBA Admissions

MBA Canada

Top Canadian MBA Programs: Rotman, Schulich, Sauder, Desautels

Canada's top MBA programs — Rotman (U of T), Schulich (York), Sauder (UBC), and Desautels (McGill) — all accept GMAT or GRE scores. Application deadlines range from November through April for September intake. Strong GMAT/GRE scores remain critical for top programs.

Research Programs

Canadian Graduate Schools Expanding Research Opportunities

Canadian universities are increasing graduate research funding, especially in AI, sustainability, and health sciences. Universities like U of T, UBC, and Waterloo are competing globally for top research students. Strong GRE scores and research experience are key differentiators.

Scholarships

Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships — Apply by November 2025

The Vanier CGS program offers $50,000/year for 3 years to doctoral students at Canadian universities. Nominations are made by universities, not directly by students. Check with your target institution's graduate office well in advance of application deadlines.
