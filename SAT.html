What Is the SAT?

The SAT is a standardized exam used for undergraduate college admissions in the US and increasingly accepted worldwide. Scores range from 400–1600, and a strong result can unlock scholarships and open doors at elite institutions. The exam moved to a fully digital, adaptive format in 2024 — making smart strategy more important than ever.

Reading & Writing

54 Questions · 64 Minutes

Two adaptive modules covering reading comprehension, rhetorical analysis, grammar, punctuation, and evidence-based synthesis. Score: 200–800.

Math

44 Questions · 70 Minutes

Two adaptive modules covering algebra, advanced functions, geometry, statistics, and data analysis. Calculator permitted throughout. Score: 200–800.

Adaptive Scoring

Module 2 Adjusts to You

Module 2 difficulty is set by your Module 1 performance. Performing well in Module 1 routes you to higher-ceiling questions — and a higher possible score.

Total Score

400–1600 · Offered 7× Per Year

The full exam runs 2 hours 14 minutes. Students can retake and submit their best score to most universities, giving you multiple chances to hit your target.

SAT Tutoring FAQ

How many sessions will I need to see improvement?
Most students see meaningful score improvements within 8–12 weeks of consistent sessions. The exact number depends on your starting score, your target, and how frequently we meet. After your diagnostic assessment Neil will give you a realistic, honest timeline — no inflated package upselling.
Do you tutor in-person or online?
Both. Neil tutors students in-person in Toronto and the GTA, and online worldwide via video call. The approach and results are equally strong in both formats.
What score improvement can I realistically expect?
On average, Neil's students achieve 100–150 point SAT improvements. Results vary based on the student's starting point, commitment to practice between sessions, and the time available before test day. Students who start further from their target and commit to regular practice tend to see the largest gains.
How is this different from an SAT prep course or book?
Courses and books cover everything for everyone. Neil covers exactly what you need — no more, no less. Every session is built around your error patterns from the diagnostic and subsequent practice. That targeted efficiency is why Neil's students consistently outperform those who relied on self-study or generic programs.
When should my student start SAT prep?
Ideally 3–6 months before the target test date. This allows enough time for genuine skill-building rather than cramming. For students aiming at top US universities, starting in Grade 10 or early Grade 11 gives the most flexibility to sit the test multiple times.
Do you also help with the ACT, GRE, and GMAT?
Yes. In addition to the SAT, Neil tutors the ACT (averaging 5–10 point gains), GRE, and GMAT (averaging 70–100 point improvements). If you're unsure whether the SAT or ACT is better suited for your student, Neil can advise during the free consultation.
