What Is the ACT?

The ACT is a curriculum-based college admissions exam accepted by all US universities and many institutions worldwide. Scores range from 1–36, and the ACT's distinct four-section structure — including an optional Science section — makes it a stronger fit for many students than the SAT.

English

75 Questions · 45 Minutes

Tests grammar, punctuation, sentence structure, and rhetorical skills. The section rewards speed and precision — with 45 seconds per question on average.

Mathematics

60 Questions · 60 Minutes

Covers pre-algebra through trigonometry and basic statistics. A full calculator is allowed throughout — but question difficulty escalates quickly toward the end.

Reading

40 Questions · 35 Minutes

Four passages drawn from literary narrative, social science, humanities, and natural science. Time management is critical — under a minute per question.

Science

40 Questions · 35 Minutes

Despite the name, this section tests data interpretation and reasoning — not memorized science facts. Students who learn the patterns score significantly better.

ACT Tutoring FAQ

Is the ACT harder than the SAT?
Neither is objectively harder — they test different things in different ways. The ACT moves faster (especially English and Reading) and includes a Science section. The SAT uses an adaptive digital format and goes deeper on algebra and data analysis. Most students have a natural fit for one or the other, which Neil can help identify through a short diagnostic.
How many sessions will I need?
Most students see meaningful improvement within 8–12 weeks of consistent sessions. Students aiming for a 3–5 point gain often need fewer sessions than those targeting a 7–10 point jump. After the diagnostic, Neil gives you a realistic, honest timeline with no pressure to commit to a large package.
Do I need to know science for the ACT Science section?
No — and this surprises most students. The ACT Science section tests your ability to read graphs, interpret data, and compare experimental results. It does not test biology, chemistry, or physics content. Once students understand this, their Science scores typically improve faster than any other section.
Do you tutor in-person or online?
Both. Neil tutors students in-person in Toronto and the GTA, and online worldwide via video call. The approach and results are equally strong in both formats.
Can I take both the SAT and ACT?
Yes, and some students do. However, most students get better results by focusing their prep on one exam. After a diagnostic session Neil can advise which test is the better strategic choice for your specific profile and university targets.
Do you also help with SAT, GRE, and GMAT?
Yes. In addition to the ACT, Neil tutors the SAT (averaging 100–150 point gains), GRE, and GMAT (averaging 70–100 point improvements). All four tests can be discussed during the free consultation.
